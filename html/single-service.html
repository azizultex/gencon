Design Build

Not all projects should be Design Build. However, the current market demands timely delivery and cost management that can make the processes extremely beneficial to the client. A lot of our clients are fast growing companies that need to make smart, educated real estate moves in a timely manner to remain competitive.

This is where GenCon is most effective. We can simplify the paperwork process by becoming fully accountable for the Design and Build components. We set a budget by creating a design and schedule with the end users and manage the process seamlessly throughout.

Your design-build team is jointly involved in producing a product that meets a client’s needs rather than merely acting as professional advisors. Collaboration of designers and builders is the foundation of design-build.

Benefits of design-build project delivery include:

Total responsibility for the design and construction entrusted to one entity — no divided responsibilities with resultant problems.

A coordinated team approach. The Design-Build team works with your management and engineering personnel to identify your needs and provide counsel on important decisions that can affect design, performance and cost.

The design team has access to accurate cost information. We build the projects as well as design them, so we have accurate costs based on actual experience.

Design, construction and cost knowledge are focused in a cooperative team effort on developing a satisfactory building solution, limiting the risks of communication failures and conflicts of interests between the designer, contractor and owner — and fragmented construction responsibility.

Better control of design, cost and construction. You limit the risks of omissions, errors and costs exceeding your budgets. Costs are known and predictable.

Faster occupancy and facilities operation — the cooperative team effort permits you to accomplish tasks faster. You save design, engineering and construction time. Many tasks under the coordinated team effort can be performed concurrently.

Design-Build Project Delivery Method:

  • Accounts for nearly 40% of nonresidential design and construction.
  • Decreases an owner’s administrative burden via the single point of responsibility contract; this way owners can focus on the project rather than managing disparate contracts.
  • Virtually eliminates litigation claims for owners.
  • Optimizes the use of innovations such as BIM and Lean concepts.

Design Build Services include:
